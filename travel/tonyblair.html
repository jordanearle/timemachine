THE GUARDIAN 02/05/97

Blair's crushing triumph: Majority is projected at over 180

Tony Blair swept into Downing Street early today on the crest of an historic Labour wave which looked set to deliver him a record post-war majority and the Tories their most humiliating electoral defeat since 1906.

The Foreign Secretary, Michael Portillo, darling of the Thatcherite right - who described it as "a truly terrible night for the Tories" was among the casualties of the most extraordinary political landslide of modern times.

"At 3.30am John Major, who easily held his own seat, revealed he had telephoned Mr Blair an hour earlier to concede defeat and congratulate the youngest prime minister since 1812, 44 next week.

But it was Mr Blair's night, an extraordinary vindication of his single-minded drive for power since succeeding John Smith in 1994.
